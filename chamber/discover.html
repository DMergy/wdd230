Discover Seaside!

A few interesting stats about Seaside:

  • Population (2020): 6,811
  • 84th Largest City in Oregon
  • Median Age: 49.4
  • Median Household Income: $41,625
  • Median Property Value: $317,700
  • Growth Rate of 0.91% Annually
  • Average Work Commute of 18.4 minutes


Before European settlement, the land that is now Seaside was home to the Clatsop Tribe. In November of 1805, Lewis and Clark reached the Pacific Ocean and set up camp north of Seaside by the mouth of the Columbia River. In January of 1806, some of the expedition traveled south and set up a salt works. Today, you can visit a re-creation on the exact location of the original salt works.


An option for sightseeing around the Seaside area includes hiking one or all of the many trails available. Get back to nature and see wondrous views of our scenic landscapes. Start at our famous Tillamook Head Trailhead which will lead you to your choice of paths along the way. Explore one, explore them all. This hiking area is a designated National Recreation Trail and part of the Oregon Coast Trail.


Another favorite way to get around Seaside is to rent a bike of your choice from one of our downtown businesses. Bike around town without worrying about parking and you can even rent special bikes to peddle along the sandy beaches. Our local rentals have options, group bikes, electric bikes, solo bikes, etc. Start at Wheel Fun Rentals and they will help guide you to the perfect bike(s) for your exploration plans.


Last but not least, be sure to schedule in some time at our local beach. Enjoy the sand and the sea with many or find a quiet spot alone. We have quite a few hotels and rentals a short walk from the stretch of sand lining our western shores. Lanai at the Cove has its own private beach, be sure to check out their rooms.
